You have questions.  We have answers.


Why does Forging Friendships exist?

The goal of Forging Friendships is to enable special needs children to have meaningful friendships beyond the home and classroom.  Our hope is to create a conscious shift in the way special needs children are viewed by others, through our events.

What is a Forging Friendship Event?

These events are where children with intellectual and physical disabilities come together to meet traditional children and other children with disabilities.

Each event has various tables called “learning centers” which allow children to explore different activities.  The traditional children will manage the table, while the special needs children explore each activity. Ultimately, both types of children will play and learn from each other. 

The traditional children, will go through a disability awareness program called Empathy in Action before running any learning center.

What about the special needs parent?

A Forging Friendship Event is a collaborative process in that the special needs child is brought to the event by their parent(s) or guardian who accompanies them as they explore each learning center.

As a parent of a special needs child you are an important part of the friendship event; you too have the opportunity to meet other parents with special needs children in a friendly and comfortable setting.

What role do the traditional children play?

The traditional children would take part in our Empathy in Action program to become certified volunteers, called “Kindness Ambassadors” ™.

Through these programs the traditional child will fully understand how it would feel to have an intellectual or physical disability and gain a deeper understanding of the challenges that their disabled peers face.

The Kindness Ambassadors will also work as a team to help spearhead each activity at the Forging Friendship Events and in doing so learn foundational leadership and project management skills.

These young volunteers will become empathetic, strong leaders by advocating for genuine friendship, disability awareness and ultimately acceptance.

Empathy in Action

The Empathy in Action program is designed to better understand how it feels to be a young person with a disability.  It addresses the overall fears and misconceptions about children with intellectual and physical disabilities.

This educational program provides the opportunity to learn what to say and how to best ask questions to a disabled peer, resulting in a positive interaction.
